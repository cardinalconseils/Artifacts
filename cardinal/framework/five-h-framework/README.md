# The Five H Framework — A Leadership Philosophy for Real AI Adoption

## 1. The Promise

After working through the Five H Framework, a leader has an honest diagnosis of where their *own* AI adoption is shallow — not their team's, theirs — and a 90-day path to close it. Concretely, they walk out with: a personal AI ROI baseline measured on three metrics, three to five daily workflows re-engineered around AI, a handoff-and-governance map for their team, and a Leader AI Impact Dashboard they can take to their board.

The deliverable isn't more tools. It's the answer to one question: **Is AI making me a better leader, or just a busier one?** — backed by their own before/after data.

## 2. The Strategy

**Where we play:** Quebec and Canadian leaders — delivered in French or English — who *believe* they've already adopted AI and are willing to be challenged on that. Single-leader engagements. No team required to start. We do **not** play in the "here are 50 AI tools to try" market, and we disqualify any leader looking for a vendor-affiliated tool recommendation list.

**How we win:** The bottleneck isn't access — 88% of companies already use AI; only 6–7% are winning. The gap is adoption *depth*, and depth is a coaching problem, not a tooling one. Organizations that combine coaching with training see 88% productivity gains; training alone delivers 22%. We attack the highest-leverage point in any organization — the leader's own thinking process — because decisions cascade. A leader who changes how they decide changes everything downstream of them.

**What we do better:** Four things competitors don't combine. (1) It's a *philosophy*, not a toolbox — no vendor affiliations. (2) It starts with the *leader*, not the org — most AI consulting goes top-down into processes. (3) It's *bilingual* and built for Quebec/Canadian market realities. (4) It's *anti-bullshit by design* — no hype, no AGI predictions, no future-of-work slides. Just: here's what's measurably true, here's your gap, here's how to close it.

**Strategic principle:** Leaders are confident and blind at the same time. Gartner found 68% of leaders say they keep pace with AI, yet 93% report their teams lack the skills to execute. You cannot close a gap you can't see. The framework's entire job is to make the invisible gap measurable — and a measured gap is a coachable gap.

## 4. The Core Framework — The Five H's

Five lenses for how a leader adopts AI — not as a tool stack, but as a way of working and thinking. Each H names a blindspot, the work it covers, and the single question the leader must answer.

**H1 — Human · *You are the constraint.***
Most leaders optimize their team's AI use before their own. That's backwards. The highest-leverage AI investment in any organization is the leader's own thinking process, because decisions cascade.
*The question:* **Where do I slow things down because I haven't changed how I work?**

**H2 — Horizon · *You're optimizing for now.***
Tactical adoption — AI for today's tasks — misses the strategic shift 18 months out. Horizon builds foresight capacity: compressing scenario planning, competitive analysis, and signal detection from weeks to hours.
*The question:* **What would I decide differently if I could run 10x more scenarios before committing?**

**H3 — Habits · *Adoption is behavior change, not willpower.***
AI integration fails at the habit layer. Leaders try it, find it useful, then revert under pressure. That's not laziness — it's how habits work. New behavior requires friction removal and reinforcement, not motivation.
*The question:* **What do I do every day that I'd never accept doing manually if there was a better way?**

**H4 — Handoff · *You're the bottleneck for your team.***
A leader who adopts AI personally but doesn't redesign how the team works around AI just creates a new bottleneck. Handoff is trust architecture: what to delegate to AI-augmented processes, what to delegate to humans, what to keep.
*The question:* **What am I holding that I should be delegating — and what am I delegating that I should be governing?**

**H5 — Harvest · *You're measuring activity, not impact.***
Most leaders track adoption by volume: how many tools, users, prompts. That's activity. Harvest measures the right things — speed of decision, quality of output, reduction in cognitive load, and business outcomes tied to AI integration.
*The question:* **What would I need to measure to know whether AI is making me a better leader — or just a busier one?**

The five run in sequence for a reason: you can't redesign team handoffs (H4) before your own habits are stable (H3), and you can't build habits (H3) before you know your constraint (H1) and where the horizon demands change (H2). Harvest (H5) is last because you measure what you changed, not what you intended to change.

## 5. The Implementation Path — The 90-Day Engagement

The Five H Framework is the diagnostic spine of a 90-day AI leadership coaching engagement. Three phases, sequenced. Every leader's constraint profile is different — no generic frameworks get applied.

### Phase 1 — Weeks 1–3: Human + Horizon (Baseline)

**Step 1 — Constraint diagnosis (H1)**
Map how the leader currently processes information and where cognitive load is highest. Separate tasks they *should* do themselves from tasks they *habitually* do themselves.
*Produces:* A personal constraint profile · *Time:* Week 1

**Step 2 — Horizon scan (H2)**
Map the industry's AI trajectory (not competitors' tool stacks). Identify the 2–3 decisions that will matter most in the next 18 months and set them up to be pressure-tested with AI.
*Produces:* A shortlist of high-stakes decisions + an 18-month signal map · *Time:* Weeks 2–3

**Step 3 — Baseline the metrics (H5, set early)**
Capture the before-state on three key metrics so Harvest in Phase 3 has something to compare against. You cannot prove ROI you didn't baseline.
*Produces:* The personal AI ROI baseline · *Time:* End of Week 3

### Phase 2 — Weeks 4–8: Habits + Handoff (Integration Sprint)

**Step 4 — Habit installation (H3)**
Identify the 3–5 daily leader workflows with highest AI ROI. Design frictionless entry points — no new apps, no long prompts, just integration into the existing flow. Run the 21-day habit lock-in protocol.
*Produces:* 3–5 locked daily AI-augmented habits · *Time:* Weeks 4–6

**Step 5 — Team workflow redesign (H4)**
Redesign team workflows *around* AI capabilities — not AI bolted on top of old processes. Build team AI fluency without the leader becoming the IT department.
*Produces:* Redesigned workflows + a fluency plan · *Time:* Weeks 6–7

**Step 6 — Governance install (H4)**
Define what decisions need a human in the loop and where autonomous AI execution is acceptable. This is the trust architecture that prevents the leader from becoming the bottleneck.
*Produces:* A human-in-the-loop governance map · *Time:* Week 8

### Phase 3 — Weeks 9–12: Harvest (Measurement & Lock-In)

**Step 7 — Measure the impact (H5)**
Re-measure the three baseline metrics. Stand up the Leader AI Impact Dashboard. Quantify speed of decision, quality of output, and cognitive-load reduction against the Week 3 baseline.
*Produces:* Before/after on 3 metrics + live dashboard · *Time:* Weeks 9–10

**Step 8 — Build the business case & next horizon**
Use the leader's own data to build the internal case for deeper AI investment. Define the next horizon so momentum survives the engagement.
*Produces:* An internal business case + a next-horizon plan · *Time:* Weeks 11–12

### The Leader AI Impact Dashboard — the 5 metrics that actually matter
```
1. SPEED OF DECISION
   Baseline: avg. days from "problem surfaced" to "decision made"
   Target:   compression %, not raw count

2. QUALITY OF OUTPUT
   Baseline: rework rate / decisions revisited within 30 days
   Target:   reduction in revisits

3. COGNITIVE LOAD
   Baseline: self-rated 1–10 on "end-of-day mental fatigue"
   Target:   sustained reduction across the engagement

4. SCENARIO THROUGHPUT  (Horizon)
   Baseline: # of options considered before a major decision
   Target:   10x more scenarios pressure-tested pre-commit

5. DELEGATION RATIO  (Handoff)
   Baseline: % of recurring decisions still routed through the leader
   Target:   shift toward governed-not-held
```
> Track impact, not activity. "How many prompts" and "how many tools" are vanity metrics. None of the five above is a volume count.

```

### Handoff Governance Decision Table (H4)
```
For each recurring decision or task, place it in ONE column:

## 8. Pitfalls & Failure Modes

**Optimizing the team before the leader.** The most common and most expensive mistake. Rolling AI out to the team while the leader's own decision process is unchanged installs a new bottleneck at the top. H1 is first for a reason — fix the constraint, then cascade.

**Confusing tool use with adoption.** "I use ChatGPT" is not adoption; it's using a tool. Adoption is integrating AI into how you think, decide, and lead. A leader who can't name one decision AI changed this month has a depth gap, regardless of how many tools they've tried.

**Skipping the baseline.** If you don't capture the before-state in Week 3, Harvest has nothing to measure against and the whole engagement collapses into anecdote. No baseline, no business case.

**Treating habits as a willpower problem.** Telling a leader to "just use AI more" fails under pressure every time. Behavior change needs friction removal and reinforcement. If a habit didn't stick, the entry point had too much friction — fix the friction, don't blame the leader.

**Stacking habits.** Installing 3–5 new AI habits simultaneously guarantees all of them revert. One at a time, locked at 21 days, then the next. The protocol is sequential by design.

**Measuring activity instead of impact.** Counting tools, users, and prompts feels like progress and proves nothing. The five dashboard metrics are all impact or outcome measures — none is a volume count. If a metric goes up when you simply do *more*, it's the wrong metric.

**Selling it as a tool engagement.** The moment this becomes "let me show you 50 tools," the differentiation is dead and you're competing with every vendor. It's a philosophy and a coaching relationship. Hold that line.

## 9. Proof of Implementation

You know the engagement worked when:

- [ ] A personal AI ROI baseline exists on 3 metrics, captured by end of Week 3
- [ ] The leader can name the single biggest constraint in their own thinking process (H1)
- [ ] 2–3 high-stakes 18-month decisions have been pressure-tested with 10x more scenarios (H2)
- [ ] 3–5 daily AI-augmented habits are locked at 21 days each, not stacked (H3)
- [ ] A handoff governance map exists: KEEP / human-in-the-loop / autonomous (H4)
- [ ] The Leader AI Impact Dashboard shows before/after deltas, not activity counts (H5)
- [ ] An internal business case for deeper AI investment exists, built on the leader's own data

**Not done if:**
- The team got AI training but the leader's own decision process is unchanged (optimized the wrong layer)
- The dashboard tracks tool count or prompt volume (measuring activity, not impact)
- Habits were all installed at once (they'll revert — re-run H3 sequentially)
- There's no Week 3 baseline (impossible to prove the engagement moved anything)

## 10. Next Level

1. **Cascade to the leadership team.** Once the leader has changed their own working patterns, run a lighter Five H pass with their direct reports. The leader now models it instead of mandating it — adoption that comes from the top because the top changed first.
2. **Quarterly Harvest re-runs.** AI capability and the leader's role both drift. Re-baseline the 5 dashboard metrics every quarter to catch regression and surface the next horizon.
3. **Productize the diagnostic.** The Five H Diagnostic (Section 7) is a standalone lead-generation asset — a self-scored gap finder that ends in a baseline conversation.
4. **Bilingual cohort delivery.** Run the framework as a small-cohort program for non-competing leaders, in French or English, preserving the single-leader depth while improving unit economics.
5. **Board-level reporting layer.** Roll the Leader AI Impact Dashboard up into a board narrative: "here's what measurably changed in how we decide," replacing vanity adoption stats with outcome metrics.

### Worked Example 1 — The "I already test things myself" CEO

**Input:** A mid-market CEO who uses ChatGPT daily and believes she's an AI adopter.
**H1 diagnosis:** She personally writes every board summary — 6 hours a month of high-cognitive-load work she habitually keeps.
**H3 habit:** A saved board-summary prompt anchored to her existing Friday review; entry under 60 seconds.
**H5 result:** Board prep drops from 6 hours to 90 minutes; cognitive-load self-rating falls 3 points. The "adopter" discovers she'd adopted a *tool*, not a *practice*.

### Worked Example 2 — The bottleneck VP

**Input:** A VP of Operations whose team waits on him to approve routine vendor decisions.
**H4 diagnosis:** 70% of recurring decisions still route through him; nothing is governed, everything is held.
**Handoff map:** Routine vendor approvals → autonomous with monthly audit; risk-flagged ones → human-in-the-loop.
**H5 result:** Delegation ratio shifts from 30% to 75% governed-not-held; decision speed for his team improves measurably without quality loss.

### Worked Example 3 — The tactical-only founder

**Input:** A founder using AI for today's tasks but blind to the 18-month shift in his category.
**H2 diagnosis:** Zero scenario work before major bets; competitive analysis done once a year.
**Horizon practice:** AI as a strategic sounding board — 10x scenario throughput on the 2 decisions that matter most.
**H5 result:** A pricing decision he'd have made on instinct gets pressure-tested across 12 scenarios in an afternoon, surfacing a downside he'd missed entirely.

### How to Defend the IP

**Proprietary:** The Five H structure (Human → Horizon → Habits → Handoff → Harvest) and its sequencing logic. The "you are the constraint" leader-first ordering. The pairing of each H with a single diagnostic question. The Leader AI Impact Dashboard (the specific 5 impact metrics) and the 21-day habit lock-in protocol as applied to leader workflows.

**Commodity:** AI coaching generally. Habit-formation science. Playing-to-Win strategy language. Generic "human in the loop" governance talk.

**Defense:** The Five H is defensible because it's a *sequenced diagnostic spine*, not a tip list — the order is load-bearing (you can't run H4 before H3 is stable) and the leader-first axiom inverts the standard top-down-into-process consulting motion.
