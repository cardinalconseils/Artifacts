H1
Human · The blindspot

You are the constraint

Most leaders optimize their team's AI use before their own. That's backwards. The highest-leverage AI investment in any organization is the leader's own thinking process — because decisions cascade.

  • Self-awareness of how you currently process information
  • Identifying where cognitive load is highest — where AI augmentation has the most impact
  • Separating tasks you should do yourself from tasks you habitually do yourself
The question every leader must answer

Where do I slow things down because I haven't changed how I work?

H2
Horizon · The blindspot

You're optimizing for now

Leaders who adopt AI tactically — for today's tasks — miss the strategic shift happening 18 months out. Horizon builds foresight capacity: compressing scenario planning, competitive analysis, and signal detection from weeks to hours.

  • Using AI as a strategic sounding board, not just a task executor
  • Mapping your industry's AI trajectory — not just your competitors' tool stack
  • Identifying the 2–3 decisions that will matter most in the next 18 months — and pressure-testing them now
The question every leader must answer

What would I decide differently if I could run 10x more scenarios before committing?

H3
Habits · The blindspot

Adoption requires behavior change, not willpower

AI integration fails at the habit layer. Leaders try it, find it useful, then revert to old patterns under pressure. That's not laziness — it's how habits work. New behavior requires friction removal and reinforcement, not motivation.

  • Identifying the 3–5 daily leader workflows where AI integration has the highest ROI
  • Designing frictionless entry points — no new apps, no long prompts, just integrated into existing flow
  • The 21-day habit lock-in protocol for AI-augmented decision-making
The question every leader must answer

What do I do every day that I'd never accept doing manually if there was a better way?

H4
Handoff · The blindspot

You're the bottleneck for your team

Leaders who personally adopt AI but don't redesign how their team works around AI just create a new bottleneck. Handoff is trust architecture: what to delegate to AI-augmented processes, what to delegate to humans, and what to keep.

  • Redesigning team workflows around AI capabilities — not adding AI on top of old processes
  • Building team AI fluency without becoming the IT department
  • Governance: what decisions need a human in the loop, and where autonomous AI execution is acceptable
The question every leader must answer

What am I holding that I should be delegating — and what am I delegating that I should be governing?

H5
Harvest · The blindspot

You're measuring activity, not impact

Most leaders track AI adoption by volume: how many tools, how many users, how many prompts. That's activity. Harvest measures the right things — speed of decision, quality of output, reduction in cognitive load, and business outcomes tied to AI integration.

  • Defining your personal AI ROI baseline (before/after on 3 key metrics)
  • The Leader AI Impact Dashboard: 5 metrics that actually matter
  • Building the business case for deeper AI investment using your own data
The question every leader must answer

What would I need to measure to know whether AI is making me a better leader — or just a busier one?

The 21-Day Habit Lock-In Protocol (H3)
PICK         1 daily leader workflow, highest AI ROI, lowest friction
ANCHOR       Attach it to an existing daily action (no new app, no new slot)
SHRINK       Reduce the entry to <60 seconds — a saved prompt, not a blank box
TRACK        Binary yes/no per day for 21 days
REINFORCE    Weekly coaching check: what broke, what stuck, remove friction
LOCK         Day 21 — only then add the next workflow. Never stack two at once.

What kills the engagement

Pitfalls & failure modes

1Optimizing the team before the leader▶
The most common and most expensive mistake. Rolling AI out to the team while the leader's own decision process is unchanged installs a new bottleneck at the top. H1 is first for a reason — fix the constraint, then cascade.
2Confusing tool use with adoption▶
"I use ChatGPT" is not adoption; it's using a tool. Adoption is integrating AI into how you think, decide, and lead. A leader who can't name one decision AI changed this month has a depth gap, regardless of how many tools they've tried.
3Skipping the baseline▶
If you don't capture the before-state in Week 3, Harvest has nothing to measure against and the whole engagement collapses into anecdote. No baseline, no business case.
4Treating habits as a willpower problem▶
Telling a leader to "just use AI more" fails under pressure every time. Behavior change needs friction removal and reinforcement. If a habit didn't stick, the entry point had too much friction — fix the friction, don't blame the leader.
5Stacking habits▶
Installing 3–5 new AI habits simultaneously guarantees all of them revert. One at a time, locked at 21 days, then the next. The protocol is sequential by design.
6Measuring activity instead of impact▶
Counting tools, users, and prompts feels like progress and proves nothing. The five dashboard metrics are all impact or outcome measures — none is a volume count. If a metric goes up when you simply do more, it's the wrong metric.
7Selling it as a tool engagement▶
The moment this becomes "let me show you 50 tools," the differentiation is dead and you're competing with every vendor. It's a philosophy and a coaching relationship. Hold that line.
